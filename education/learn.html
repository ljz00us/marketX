the Basics

What is equity?
Equity is stock. Stock is just a piece of paper entitling you to a portion of the company’s profit every year.


Right. So what is it exactly?
Say you wanted to set up a lemonade stand. All you need to buy is a table, a jug, a few lemons, a few cups, and paper for a sign. So your costs are around $50.



Now, if you sold each cup of lemonade for $5, after 10 customers, you’d make $50 in revenue. Which is great. So now your revenue equals your costs.

If you sold one more glass of lemonade, you just made profit a $5 profit. That's awesome.


But what if I had shareholders?
Ok so let's say instead you begged your parents for money and they gave you $50.

Now, things are different. That $5 profit partly belongs to them. Because they are now your shareholders.
